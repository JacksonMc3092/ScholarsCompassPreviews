I. Visual and Digital Texts Make Arguments

College writing does not only respond to printed articles. In ENG 1010, you may analyze advertisements, videos, maps, social media feeds, websites, screenshots, platform recommendations, campus spaces, or public designs. These texts persuade through image, sound, layout, sequence, repetition, and interaction.

A visual or digital text may not state its argument in a thesis sentence. Instead, it may guide your attention, make one choice feel obvious, center one kind of person, hide another, or make a behavior feel normal.

Key Takeaway

When you analyze a visual or digital text, describe what is there first. Then explain what those choices do.

II. The Describe Before You Interpret Rule

Students often jump too quickly from seeing to judging. Slow down. Start with description.

MoveHow to Use It
DescribeName what you literally see or hear: colors, camera angles, sound, layout, images, text, order, repetition, buttons, captions, or recommendations.
Notice PatternAsk what repeats or stands out. One detail may be accidental; a pattern usually matters.
InterpretExplain what the choices invite the audience to feel, believe, value, ignore, or do.
Not enough: The app is addictive.
Stronger: The app's endless vertical scroll, quick videos, and constant novelty encourage users to keep moving before they have time to decide whether they want to stop.
III. What to Look For
  • Framing: What is centered? What is cropped out?
  • Sequence: What comes first, next, and last?
  • Sound: How do music, silence, voice, or sound effects shape emotion?
  • Design: What do color, spacing, size, layout, buttons, and menus encourage?
  • Repetition: What image, phrase, behavior, or recommendation keeps appearing?
  • Audience: Who is imagined as the normal user, viewer, customer, or citizen?
IV. Use This in Your Essays

Essay 1: Use this chapter to analyze ad images, music, pacing, editing, symbols, camera distance, and on-screen text.

PracticeScreenshot or scene note: Choose one visual or digital moment. Describe it in three sentences. Then write one sentence explaining what it encourages the audience to notice or value.
