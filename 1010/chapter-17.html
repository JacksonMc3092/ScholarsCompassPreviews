Peer Review and Giving Useful Feedback

Help another writer see what is working and what needs development.

  • Use this chapter to move from reading and noticing toward a stronger draft.
I. Peer Review Is Reader Response

Peer review is not editing someone else's paper into your voice. It is helping the writer understand how the draft is working for a real reader.

The best feedback is specific, honest, respectful, and focused on revision.

II. What to Look For
MoveHow to Use It
ThesisCan you tell what the draft is arguing?
EvidenceDoes the writer use specific moments, sources, examples, or observations?
ExplanationDoes the writer explain how the evidence proves the point?
OrganizationCan you follow the order of ideas?
Source UseAre sources introduced, cited, and explained?
III. Useful Feedback Language
  • "The strongest part of this draft is..."
  • "I understood your point clearly when..."
  • "I wanted more explanation after..."
  • "This paragraph seems to be making two different points..."
  • "One revision priority might be..."
Weak feedback: Good job. Add more detail.
Useful feedback: Your paragraph about music is interesting, but I need one specific scene or sound choice from the ad so I can see the evidence you mean.
IV. Peer Review Mini-Protocol
  1. State the draft's main claim in your own words.
  2. Name one strength with a specific example.
  3. Ask one real reader question.
  4. Suggest one revision priority.
